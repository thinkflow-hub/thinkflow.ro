## Why Your Space Shapes Your Output

Whether you are recording a podcast, editing video, or shipping code, your environment directly affects the quality of your work. The right home studio setup is not about expensive gear -- it is about matching the space to the workflow.

## The Podcast Studio Setup

- **Room treatment**: Start with acoustic panels at first reflection points. A closet full of clothes works as a poor man vocal booth.
- **Mic**: An SM58 or AT2020 into a Focusrite Scarlett Solo will out-perform a USB mic every time.
- **Monitor**: Treat your room before buying expensive monitors. KRK Rokits with Sonarworks correction are the sweet spot.
- **Desk**: A standing desk from Autonomous or Flexispot with a monitor arm frees up desk space for your interface and MIDI controller.

## The Video and Podcast Hybrid Studio

- **Camera**: Sony ZV-E10 on a tripod with a key light (Aputure Amaran 60x) and a fill light.
- **Acoustic treatment**: Moveable gobos (towel racks with moving blankets) for flexibility between recording and mixing.
- **Cable management**: A cheap IKEA Signum rack under the desk. Label both ends of every cable.

## The Coding War Room

- **Monitors**: Two 27 inch 4K displays (LG 27UP850W) on gas arms. The vertical monitor is for docs and terminals.
- **Chair**: Herman Miller Aeron or Steelcase Gesture -- your back will thank you after year two.
- **Desk depth**: At least 30 inches to accommodate monitor arms without being 12 inches from the screen.
- **Lighting**: Bias lighting behind monitors (Philips Hue Play) reduces eye strain during late-night sessions.

## The Budget Breakdown

| Setup Type | Min Budget | Comfortable | Pro |
|------------|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Podcast | $500 | $1,200 | $3,000+ |

The best investment you can make is a good chair, good lighting, and treated acoustics. Everything else scales with your budget.
